# Gallivant — origin reference

Canonical source for the fictional example company in this folder. Derived messaging in `canon-*.md`, `brand-*`, and `content-index.md` should trace here. **Not a real company** — demo material only, not tied to any production workspace.

---

**Gallivant**  
*Enterprise Travel & Expense Management*

---

## Alice and Becca

Alice responds to every new customer support ticket personally for the first 30 days of an account. Not a template — an actual response, often with a suggestion the customer didn't ask for but immediately realizes they needed. She has a running document of every piece of feedback Gallivant has ever received, organized by theme, with her notes on what they did about it. The document is 84 pages. She considers this normal.

Becca once drove two hours to sit with a customer's finance team in person because she felt like they weren't getting the most out of Gallivant View and she wanted to watch them use it. She brought coffee. She stayed four hours. They renewed at twice the contract size. Becca maintains this was a coincidence.

They have a shared rule: no customer leaves Gallivant without understanding exactly why, and without someone at Gallivant having genuinely tried to fix the thing that drove them away. They have recovered eleven churned customers this way. They talk about all eleven of them by first name.

---

## How they talk to customers

Directly. Warmly. With the confidence of people who have thought about expense management more than anyone reasonably should and have arrived, on the other side of that, at something genuinely useful.

They don't have a "Customer Success" team. They have a team called **Travel Companions**, which Becca named and Alice initially resisted and now defends fiercely to anyone who questions it.

The Travel Companions send a handwritten card when a customer goes live. Not a postcard — a card, with an envelope, with a stamp. Alice designed the card. It has a small illustration of a sandwich in the corner. It says: *Welcome to Gallivant. We're glad you're here. Let's go.*

---

## What customers say

"It just works, and when it doesn't, someone named either Alice or Becca calls me within the hour." — a real quote, from a real customer, that is currently on the Gallivant homepage because Alice asked if she could use it and the customer said yes and also asked if Alice wanted to get coffee sometime, which Alice did.

"I've never felt like software cared about me before." — another real quote, which made Becca visibly uncomfortable when it came in because she doesn't love the word "software" and feels Gallivant is more than that, but she put it on the website anyway because the customer meant it as a compliment and it was.

---

## The product, as Alice would describe it

"We built the thing we wished existed. It books your travel inside your company's policy so nobody has to ask permission for anything normal. It captures your expenses while you're still standing there so you don't have to reconstruct your week from a pile of receipts on a Sunday night. It gets your money back to you fast because you spent your own money on behalf of your company and that's not nothing. And it gives finance everything they need to see without making the person who expensed a sandwich feel like a suspect. That's it. That's Gallivant."

## The product, as Becca would describe it

"It's really fast and it works."
