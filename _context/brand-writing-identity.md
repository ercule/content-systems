# Brand writing identity (Gallivant)

How Gallivant sounds. Trace voice rules to [reference/gallivant-origin.md](reference/gallivant-origin.md). Skills read this with [brand-visual-identity.md](brand-visual-identity.md).

## Voice

**Default (Alice register):** Direct and warm. Confident without swagger — the confidence of people who have thought about expense management more than anyone reasonably should and landed on something useful. Complete sentences. Concrete examples (sandwich, Sunday night, permission for a normal flight). Willing to be slightly longer if clarity needs it.

**Short register (Becca):** "It's really fast and it works." Use for subheads, release notes lede, or when the page already explained the mechanism.

**To customers:** Talk like Travel Companions — a person who picked up the phone, not a queue. Name real roles (finance, travelers) without corporate fog.

source: gallivant-origin

## Guardrails

- Say **Travel Companions**, not Customer Success — Alice defends this name; do not substitute CS/CX jargon in customer-facing copy.
- Homepage quotes are approved verbatim only as in [canon-proof.md](canon-proof.md); do not invent similar testimonials.
- Becca prefers not to call Gallivant "software" in hero copy if a plain alternative exists ("product," "platform," or just "Gallivant"). Customer quotes that say "software" may stay when attributed.
- Do not publish the 84-page internal feedback document or name churned/recovered customers without explicit fictional clearance — founders refer to eleven recoveries by first name internally only.
- Do not promise Alice on every ticket forever — canon limit is **first 30 days of a new account**.
- Welcome card copy is fixed: *Welcome to Gallivant. We're glad you're here. Let's go.* — sandwich illustration stays in corner per Alice's design.
- No punitive language toward travelers ("non-compliant spend," "violators") — finance gets control; travelers get dignity.
